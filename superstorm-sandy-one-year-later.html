Superstorm Sandy One Year Later

Posted October 12, 2013

Superstorm Sandy hit us on Oct 29, 2012 and we were totally unprepared for reacting to such an emergency. But everyone at MCCC helped each other in any way we could, opening our homes to each other and sharing whatever we had, including electricity.

Brothers and sisters from many fellowships answered the call for help, and donated time and energy in sorting out supplies for our township by participating in Middletown Helps Its Own. A Sandy Love Fund was established. In particular we wanted to help our immediate neighbors Union Beach and Highlands, which were especially hit hard. We praise God that we collected $27,250. Our brothers and sisters were able to visit and help out with a pastor who lost his home, two different churches whose properties were severely damaged, and other brothers and sisters who had severe and urgent needs.

One year later, a visit to Union Beach showed a community that is making different kinds of decisions.

  • Some businesses have relocated.
  • Many houses are still in the process of being rebuilt,
  • Others have been razed or apparently abandoned.
  • Some are still waiting to rebuild in the future.
  • The pastor’s house is almost finished being rebuilt.
  • The church that we helped out is not completely refurbished but they can use part of their original space for their service.

Let us not forget our brothers and sisters. It will take a long time to heal the damage done by Sandy.

“… whatever you did for one of the least of these brothers and sisters of mine, you did for me. “ Mathew 25: 40
